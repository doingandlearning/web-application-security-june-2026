# News stories — Module 1 exercise

Use with the **News story → threat family** slide exercise.

Each delegate takes one story. In 5 minutes, answer:

1. **Impact** — data loss, account takeover, outage, fraud, or something else?
2. **Threat family** — injection, broken access control, auth and session flaws, or sensitive data exposure?
3. **One control** — a specific change that would have reduced the blast radius. Not "better security" — name the control.

## Story A — Easter chaos at M&S

During Easter weekend 2025, Marks & Spencer customers found they could not use click-and-collect, contactless payments failed in stores, and online ordering was suspended for six weeks. Shelves ran empty. The company's half-year profits fell 99%.

## Story B — Legal Aid website taken offline

In April 2025 the Legal Aid Agency took its digital services offline after detecting unusual activity. The agency later confirmed that personal data going back to 2007 had been accessed, including national insurance numbers, criminal records, and financial histories. Law firms reported the disruption pushed the already-stretched legal aid sector towards collapse.

### Sources

- [GOV.UK — Legal Aid Agency data breach](https://www.gov.uk/government/news/legal-aid-agency-data-breach)

- [The Guardian — Cyber-attack has pushed legal aid sector towards collapse](https://www.theguardian.com/law/2025/aug/03/legal-aid-cyber-attack-has-pushed-sector-towards-collapse-say-lawyers)

## Story C — JLR production halted for five weeks

In September 2025 a cyberattack forced Jaguar Land Rover to shut down its computer networks. Production lines at plants in Merseyside and Solihull stopped for more than a month, with workers told to stay at home. UK car production hit a 70-year low. The Bank of England cited the incident as a factor in lower-than-expected GDP growth.
